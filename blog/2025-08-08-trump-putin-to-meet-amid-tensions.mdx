**Trump and Putin to Meet Amid Tensions**

According to a recent report by BBC News, US President Donald Trump and Russian President Vladimir Putin are set to meet in the coming days, despite muted expectations for a ceasefire between Moscow and Kyiv. Trump's comments came after talks in Moscow between his envoy Steve Witkoff and Putin, which the US president described as "highly productive." The meeting comes at a time when tensions between Russia and Ukraine are high, with the US threatening to impose sweeping sanctions.

In a surprising move, Apple has partnered with Samsung to produce digital image sensors for the next year's iPhone 18 lineup, according to The Verge. The chip-making technology used in this partnership has never been used before anywhere in the world, according to Apple. This move marks a significant shift in the tech industry, where companies often compete fiercely for dominance.

**First Migrants Detained Under "One in, One Out" Deal**

On Wednesday, the first group of migrants was detained under the "one in, one out" deal with France, a pilot scheme aimed at reducing the number of asylum seekers crossing the Channel. The detentions came as people arrived in Dover, marking the first day of the scheme. Home Secretary Yvette Cooper described the move as a "ground-breaking new treaty."
